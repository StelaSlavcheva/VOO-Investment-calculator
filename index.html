üìö Financial Terms & Calculations Guide

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment)

The fixed amount you pay to the lender every month until your loan is fully repaid.

EMI = [P √ó r √ó (1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n - 1]

Principal Amount

The original loan amount you borrow from the lender, before any interest is added.

Example: If you borrow $25,000, that's your principal amount.

Interest Rate

The percentage charged by the lender on the outstanding loan amount, usually expressed as annual percentage rate (APR).

Example: 12.5% annual rate means you pay 12.5% of the remaining balance each year.

Loan Tenure

The duration over which you will repay the loan, typically measured in years.

Example: 5-year tenure = 60 monthly payments.

Total Interest

The total amount of interest you will pay over the entire loan period.

Total Interest = (EMI √ó tenure in months) - Principal Amount

Total Amount

The complete amount you will pay back, including both principal and interest.

Total Amount = Principal Amount + Total Interest

VOO ETF

Vanguard S&P 500 ETF - A fund that tracks the performance of the S&P 500 index, representing 500 of the largest US companies.

Example: Companies like Apple, Microsoft, Google are included in VOO.

Compound Interest

Interest calculated on both the initial principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods.

Future Value = Principal √ó (1 + rate)^time

Investment Profit

The gain or loss from an investment, calculated as the difference between final value and initial investment.

Profit = Investment Value - Principal Investment

Net Profit (After Loan Costs)

Investment profit minus the total interest cost of the loan used to fund the investment.

Net Profit = Investment Profit - Total Loan Interest

Cash Flow

The net amount of money moving in and out of your finances each month.

Cash Flow = Monthly Investment Profit - Monthly Loan Payment

Risk Scenarios

Different investment return assumptions based on historical performance and market conditions.

  • Conservative (7%): Lower expected returns, lower risk
  • Historical Average (10%): Based on VOO's long-term performance
  • Optimistic (15%): Higher expected returns, higher risk

üî¢ How We Calculate Everything

1. EMI Calculation (Loan Payment)

We use the standard EMI formula used by banks and financial institutions:

EMI = [P √ó r √ó (1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n - 1]

Where:

  • P = Principal Amount (loan amount)
  • r = Monthly Interest Rate (annual rate √∑ 12 √∑ 100)
  • n = Number of months (tenure in years √ó 12)

Example: $30,000 loan at 3% for 10 years

  • P = $30,000
  • r = 3% √∑ 12 √∑ 100 = 0.0025
  • n = 10 √ó 12 = 120 months
  • EMI = $289.85 per month
2. Investment Future Value

We calculate how much your investment will grow using compound interest:

Future Value = Principal √ó (1 + annual_rate)^years

Example: $30,000 invested at 10% for 10 years

  • Future Value = $30,000 √ó (1.10)^10
  • Future Value = $30,000 √ó 2.59374
  • Future Value = $77,812.20
3. Monthly Investment Profit

Estimated monthly profit from your investment:

Monthly Profit = Principal √ó (annual_rate √∑ 12)

Example: $30,000 at 10% annual rate

  • Monthly Profit = $30,000 √ó (0.10 √∑ 12)
  • Monthly Profit = $250.00 per month
4. Net Profit After Loan Costs

The most important metric - your actual profit after paying loan interest:

Net Profit = Investment Profit - Total Loan Interest

Example: Investment vs Loan comparison

  • Investment Profit: $47,812.20
  • Total Loan Interest: $4,782.00
  • Net Profit: $43,030.20 (Profit)
